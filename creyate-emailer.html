01 — Selvedge Denim Launch

Celebrating craftsmanship through
editorial email design

CREYATE launched a selvedge denim collection that champions the art of traditional denim-making — a fabric renowned for its quality, durability, and distinctive self-finished edge. The brief was to design emailer options that do justice to this craftsmanship story while driving traffic to the collection.

I designed two creative options, each rooted in CREYATE's premium aesthetic. The layout places the product and its heritage front and centre, with a restrained colour palette that lets the denim speak. Both creatives carry purposeful CTAs that reflect the brand's two key conversion touchpoints.

Header CTA — 'Shop CREYATE': Positioned at the top fold for immediate action from engaged subscribers.

Footer CTA — 'Book A Home Visit': CREYATE's signature personalised service offering, surfaced at the point of considered interest — ideal for customers wanting a bespoke experience.

02 — Blazer Ingredients Infographic

Turning product specs into
a visual story

The second emailer tackles a more complex challenge — communicating the technical craftsmanship behind the CREYATE blazer in a way that is immediately legible and visually compelling in an inbox. Product ingredient lists and spec details rarely make for engaging email content; the solution here was to treat them as infographic data.

I created a diagrammatic illustration directly overlaid onto a CREYATE lifestyle photograph — annotating the blazer's key material and construction details using graphic callouts. The result transforms a technical breakdown into an editorial infographic that feels premium rather than functional.

Design decision: Layering the illustration over the lifestyle image rather than isolating it keeps the aspirational feel of the brand intact — the craftsmanship story is told without interrupting the visual luxury of the garment.

The approach

"Every emailer is a single moment of attention in a crowded inbox. For CREYATE, the goal was to make that moment feel as considered and crafted as the garments themselves — turning product details into a visual language the brand's audience would want to engage with."
